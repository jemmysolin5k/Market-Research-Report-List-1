<p><p>The inorganic color pigments market includes several categories based on composition and properties. Lead chromates, known for their vibrant yellow and red hues, are widely used in coatings and plastics. Metal oxides, such as titanium dioxide and iron oxide, provide durable colors across various applications. Sulfides, like cadmium sulfide, offer bright pigmentation but raise safety concerns. Sulfoselenides, used for unique shades, combine sulfur and selenium elements. The "Others" category encompasses less common pigments, including those based on bismuth and antimony compounds.</p></p>
